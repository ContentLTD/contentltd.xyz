The Brief

A site that feels like a real organization

Roblox communities, especially ER:LC communities,live and die on Discord. They usually have no web presence, no identity, no permanence, aside from maybe the site they threw together on Sonoran CMS or their listing on the Melonly Directory.

ACRP needed something different. They needed a platform that felt like a real organization instead of a hobby or personal project.

The Numbers

Two years of tuning

This site was first published about two years ago, give or take, and has been improving ever since. There are roughly 10–30 pages on the site (I genuinely lost count), and I've used zero copy-paste assets.

The Hard Part

Vanilla, without the boilerplate look

Achieving something that doesn't feel like a bootstrap template, a boilerplate, or a copy-and-paste job in vanilla HTML is a lot harder than you'd think. Frameworks give you conventions and easy-to-use components (or not-easy-to-use, depending on the framework). Without them, you either impose your own, let the codebase drift, or use Shadcn…

We used Shadcn. Just kidding.

In all seriousness, after two years of tuning and redesigns, we have something that feels coherent, unique, and simple.

HTML5. CSS3. Vanilla JS.

The Outcome

A site that feels good to use

The outcome is a website that feels good to use. Hell, even I find myself sitting down for an hour just to mess with the hover effect on the "ACRP" wordmark in the nav bar.
